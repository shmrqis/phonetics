Manner of Articulation

Voiced

When the sound is voiced the glottis is closed because vocal cords are brought together. The airstream that flows through causes the cords to vibrate. Examples: /p/, /t/, /k/, /f/, /θ/, /s/.

Voiceless

When a sound is voiceless, the glottis is opened and the vocal cords are apart. This the airstream flows out without obstruction and there is no vibration of the vocal cords.. Examples: /b/, /d/, /g/, /v/, /ð/, /z/, /ʒ/.
